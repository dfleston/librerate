Technical Brief: Book Royalty Proof of Concept (PoC)1. Executive SummaryThis document outlines the technical architecture for a closed-source Proof of Concept (PoC) platform. The system facilitates the fiat purchase of "Royalty Certificates" (NFTs representing revenue-share agreements) for a single book, automates the collection of off-chain fiat royalties, and programmatically distributes on-chain USDC payouts to certificate holders.The core competitive advantage (moat) resides in the proprietary legal and operational routing, presenting a frictionless Web2 experience backed by Web3 rails.2. The Patron UX: Capital Raise & Onboarding (React + Privy + Stripe)The goal is to eliminate crypto friction. The user should not know they are creating a wallet.The "One-Step" Illusion:Technically, it is two steps happening in rapid succession under the hood, but to the user, it feels like one cohesive checkout flow.Authentication (Privy): The user clicks "Buy Royalty Certificate" on your React frontend. A minimal Privy modal prompts them for their email or Google login. In the background, Privy instantly generates an embedded wallet mapped to that user.Payment (Stripe): Immediately after entering their email, the Stripe Checkout modal appears for a standard credit card payment (e.g., $10).The Minting Trigger (Webhook): * When the payment clears, Stripe fires a checkout.session.completed webhook to your backend Node.js server.Your server reads the webhook, identifies the buyer's email, queries Privy for the newly created embedded wallet address, and fires an API call to the Smart Contract.The Smart Contract mints the Royalty Certificate to the user's invisible wallet.Result: The user paid with a card, and the system automatically created a wallet and deposited their share without them ever touching a seed phrase.3. The Backend: Institutional Banking (Kraken Embed Strategy)To scale the collection of book royalties (e.g., from Amazon KDP), the platform utilizes Kraken Embed to bridge institutional fiat to on-chain liquidity.Master Account Infrastructure: The operating company utilizes a Kraken Business/Institutional account. Through the Kraken Embed API, the company is issued a Master Account IBAN.Royalty Ingestion: This Master IBAN is provided to Amazon KDP. When Amazon settles monthly royalties, the funds land directly in the Kraken-controlled environment under the platform's corporate entity.Automated Conversion & Routing:Listening: A backend agent uses the Kraken API Key/Secret to "listen" for incoming deposits to the Master IBAN.Execution: Upon detection of fiat (EUR/USD), the agent triggers an automated market buy for USDC at institutional rates.Settlement: The USDC is programmatically withdrawn via the Kraken API directly to the Smart Contract's address on the selected network (Solana or Base).4. The Smart Contract: Logic & Payouts (Solana or Base L2)For the PoC, we use a single, easily codified smart contract deployed on a high-speed, low-cost network.Contract State & Data:Total Shares: Defined at inception (e.g., 1,000 Certificates).Ledger: The contract automatically tracks which Privy wallet addresses hold the Certificates.Distribution Logic (The Payout):The smart contract contains a distributeFunds() function.When the backend successfully sends USDC to the contract (from the Kraken conversion step), it triggers this function.The contract reads its balance, reads the ledger of certificate holders, and automatically splinters the USDC directly into the holders' wallets proportionally to their ownership.User View: The patron logs back into your website via email, opens the Privy interface, and sees their accumulated USDC balance available for withdrawal.5. Required Tech Stack & IntegrationsFrontend: Next.js (React) styled with Tailwind CSS.Authentication & Wallets: Privy SDK (@privy-io/react-auth).Payments & Webhooks: Stripe Webhooks + Stripe Node SDK.Institutional Bridge: Kraken Embed API (Staging for PoC).Backend Runtime: Node.js / Express (to listen to Stripe and Kraken webhooks).Smart Contract Network: Solana or Base (Ethereum L2).
